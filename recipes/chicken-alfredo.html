Chicken Alfredo

Chicken Alfredo is a classic Italian-American dish loved for its creamy texture and savory flavor. Tender pieces of chicken are
nestled in a rich and velvety Alfredo sauce, made with butter, cream, and Parmesan cheese, then served over a bed of al dente fettuccine noodles.
This comforting dish strikes the perfect balance between indulgence and simplicity, making it a favorite among pasta enthusiasts everywhere.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces fettuccine pasta
  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts, sliced into thin strips
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (optional, for garnish)

Steps:

  1. Cook the fettuccine pasta according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced chicken breasts and
    cook until they are no longer pink and have a golden brown color, about 5-6 minutes per side.
    Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute until fragrant.
  4. Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Allow the cream to reduce slightly, about 2-3 minutes.
  5. Stir in the grated Parmesan cheese until it is melted and the sauce is smooth.
  6. Add the cooked chicken back to the skillet and toss to coat it evenly with
    the Alfredo sauce. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Add the cooked fettuccine to the skillet and toss everything together until
    the pasta is well coated in the sauce.
  8. Serve hot, garnished with chopped parsley if desired.
